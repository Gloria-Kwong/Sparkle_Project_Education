Programs

There are many programs Sparkle Project offers for children, youth, and parents. Check them out below

LITTLE STAR

After-school programs over 15 weeks for children in grades 1-6

The program provides children with basic acting skills and character qualities learning while engaged in play. Activities include creative writing, games, drama, crafts, speeches, and movies. LITTLE STAR focuses on cooperation, teamwork, and leadership. We aim to help develop independent thinking skills that will serve these children well as they grow into young adults.

PARENT-CHILD WORKSHOP

Monthly program for parents and their children aged 6-14

We emphasize the importance of a healthy family environment in a child’s growth. The Workshop offers theatrical games to the parent and the child on a monthly basis that aim to solve conflicts and promote a mutual understanding between the kid and their parents. The family is assigned take-home exercises after each session to enhance the mentioned skills.

VOLUNTEER

High school leadership program for youth in grades 7-12

The Volunteer program is designed to help youth master the skills and character qualities they need to become leaders in their community. Through participation in event planning, classroom assistance and organization, youth earn community service hours and practice important community engagement.

SPARKLE CAMP

Summer camp over a 7-days period for kids aged 6-14

Sparkle Camp focuses on a micro-film experiment over a period of seven days, in which children take on the role of director, playwright, actors, technicians, musicians, and other crew members while making new friends, and building teamwork and leadership. Professional and caring staff equips campers with the knowledge and character required in the filmmaking process.

MULTILINGUAL THEATRE

We aim to be inclusive of arts from a variety of cultures

The Multilingual Theatre serves a larger community to create cultural diversity as the increased amount of immigration from all around the world. Audiences and performers can choose scripts from different languages based on their interests. Students can also learn from different art histories by learning a new language. Our past productions include languages in English, French, and Mandarin.

ANNUAL SHOWCASE

Seasonal productions in the theatre with ticket sales

The duration of every semester ends with a large-scale performance where students invite their family and friends to watch their production. The showcase includes a major play, a talk show, a multilingual play, and a parent-participated show. This is the time to celebrate the hard work of students over the year and enjoy their talents.
